Signs of Termite Infestation

Termites work silently and continuously, often causing extensive damage before they're discovered. Watch for these warning signs:

  • Mud tubes on foundation walls or wooden structures
  • Discarded wings near windows or doors
  • Wood that sounds hollow when tapped
  • Small holes in drywall or wood surfaces
  • Frass (termite droppings) near wooden areas
  • Buckling paint or wood that appears damaged
  • Swarmers (winged termites) emerging from walls or foundations

Types of Termites in Encinitas

Subterranean Termites

Subterranean termites are the most common and destructive type in the Encinitas area. They live underground and require contact with soil to survive. These termites build mud tubes to travel above ground and access food sources. Their colonies can contain up to 2 million members, making them capable of causing extensive damage quickly.

Drywood Termites

Drywood termites don't require soil contact and can establish colonies in dry wood above ground. They're commonly found in wooden furniture, framing, and structural timbers. While their colonies are smaller than subterranean termites, they can still cause significant damage over time.

Formosan Termites

Formosan termites are a particularly aggressive species that has been found in Southern California. They establish massive colonies and can cause damage faster than other species. Our technicians are trained to identify and treat Formosan termite infestations.
