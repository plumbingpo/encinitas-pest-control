Liquid Soil Treatments

Liquid termiticides are applied around your home's foundation to create a continuous chemical barrier. This prevents subterranean termites from entering your home from the soil. Modern non-repellent termiticides are undetectable to termites—they walk through the treated zone and carry the product back to the colony, eliminating it from within. This is our most popular treatment for active subterranean infestations and new construction pre-treatments.

Termite Bait Systems

Bait stations are installed in the soil around your property at regular intervals. Worker termites find the bait, feed on it, and share it with the colony through their social feeding behavior (trophallaxis). The active ingredient slowly eliminates the entire colony, including the queen. Bait systems are ideal for ongoing monitoring and protection, and are an excellent choice for properties where liquid treatments aren't practical.

Direct Wood Treatment

For drywood termites that live entirely within the wood they infest, we inject termiticide directly into infested timbers and apply borate-based products to vulnerable wood surfaces. This targets drywood termite colonies in place and provides long-term residual protection against future infestations.

Fumigation Referrals

For severe, widespread drywood termite infestations that have spread throughout a structure, whole-structure fumigation may be necessary. We work with licensed fumigation companies and can coordinate the entire process for you, including pre- and post-fumigation inspections.

Types of Termites in Encinitas

Subterranean Termites

Subterranean termites are the most destructive termite species in the Encinitas area. They live in underground colonies that can contain up to 2 million members and build distinctive mud tubes to travel between their soil nests and above-ground food sources. These pencil-width tubes are often visible on foundation walls, pier blocks, and in crawl spaces.

Subterranean termites require contact with soil moisture to survive, which is why they build mud tubes—to maintain humidity as they travel. They cause more structural damage than any other insect in the United States and can compromise your home's framing, subfloor, and structural timbers if left untreated.

Drywood Termites

Unlike subterranean termites, drywood termites don't need soil contact. They establish colonies directly inside dry wood above ground—in your home's framing, furniture, door frames, window frames, and even picture frames. Their colonies are smaller (typically a few thousand members) but they can still cause significant damage over time.

Drywood termites are identified by their distinctive fecal pellets (frass)—tiny, six-sided pellets that they push out of small "kick-out holes" in infested wood. Finding small piles of what looks like coarse sand or sawdust near wooden structures is a classic sign of drywood termite activity.

Formosan Termites

Formosan termites are an aggressive invasive species that has been found in parts of Southern California. They build the largest colonies of any termite species—potentially containing millions of workers—and can cause catastrophic structural damage in a relatively short time. While less common than native species in Encinitas, they are spreading and pose a growing threat to our area.

Signs of a Termite Infestation

Termites work silently and continuously, often causing extensive damage before they're discovered. Early detection is critical to minimizing damage and repair costs. Watch for these warning signs:

  • Mud tubes on foundations: Pencil-width tubes of mud on foundation walls, pier blocks, or in crawl spaces indicate subterranean termites traveling between soil and wood.
  • Discarded wings: Piles of small, translucent wings near windows, doors, or light fixtures—left behind after termite swarmers (reproductives) mate and shed their wings.
  • Hollow-sounding wood: Tap on door frames, baseboards, and window sills. Wood that sounds hollow or papery may be damaged internally by termites.
  • Frass (termite droppings): Tiny, six-sided pellets found in small piles below infested wood are a telltale sign of drywood termites.
  • Blistering or bubbling paint: Paint that appears to blister or bubble on wood surfaces can indicate moisture from termite activity beneath the surface.
  • Tight-fitting doors and windows: As termites consume wood and produce moisture, door frames and window frames can warp, making them difficult to open or close.
  • Swarmers: Winged termites emerging from walls, foundations, or soil—especially on warm days after rain—indicate a mature colony nearby.

Termite Prevention for Encinitas Homeowners

While regular professional inspections are your best defense against termites, these preventive measures can reduce your risk:

  • Eliminate wood-to-soil contact: Ensure that wood siding, door frames, and structural timbers don't directly contact soil. Maintain at least 6 inches of clearance between soil and wood.
  • Fix moisture problems: Repair leaky pipes, faucets, and air conditioning units. Ensure proper drainage away from your foundation. Termites are attracted to moisture.
  • Ventilate crawl spaces: Adequate ventilation reduces moisture buildup in crawl spaces, making them less attractive to subterranean termites.
  • Store wood away from your home: Keep firewood, lumber, and mulch at least 20 feet from your foundation and elevated off the ground.
  • Seal cracks in your foundation: Fill cracks and gaps in your foundation and around utility penetrations to eliminate entry points.
  • Schedule annual inspections: Professional termite inspections can catch infestations early, before significant damage occurs.
